The row space of
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 2 \amp 1 \\ 4 \amp 2 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
can be written as
\begin{equation*} \{ c_1 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 2 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix} + c_2 \begin{bmatrix} 4 \amp 2 \end{bmatrix} \; | \; c_1, c_2 \in \mathbb{R} \} \end{equation*}
However, since the two vectors are not linearly independent, the row space can be written as
\begin{equation*} \{ c_1 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 2 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix}\; | \; c_1 \in \mathbb{R} \} \end{equation*}
Therefore the row rank is 1.
