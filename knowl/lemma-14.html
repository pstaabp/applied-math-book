Lemma 3.2.8.

Let \(A\) be an \(m\) by \(n\) matrix. The null space of \(A\) is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\)

Proof.

We will use lemma LemmaÂ 3.2.5 to solve this. Let both \(\vec{x}\) and \(\vec{y}\) be in the null space of \(A\text{.}\) This means that \(A\vec{x}=\vec{0}\) and \(A\vec{y}=\vec{0}\text{.}\) We need to show that \(r_1 \vec{x} + r_2 \vec{y}\) is in the null space of \(A\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} A(r_1 \vec{x} + r_2 \vec{y}) \amp = r_1 A\vec{x} + r_2 A\vec{y} \\ \amp = r_1 (0) + r_2 (0) = 0 \end{align*}
Vectors in the null space are vectors of length \(n\text{,}\) so the null space is a subset of \(\mathbb{R}^n\) and since \(r_1 \vec{x} + r_2 \vec{y}\) is in the null space of \(A\text{,}\) then the null space is a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\)
