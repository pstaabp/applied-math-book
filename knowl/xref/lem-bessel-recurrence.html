Lemma 7.7.3.

Let \(n\geq 1\) for \(n\) an integer and \(x > 0\text{,}\) then
\begin{align} x(J_{n-1}+J_{n+1}) \amp = 2n J_n\tag{7.7.5}\\ J_{n-1} - J_{n+1} \amp = 2 J'_n \tag{7.7.6} \end{align}

Proof.

If we use the product rule to expand ((7.7.3)) and ((7.7.4)), we get
\begin{align*} n x^{n-1} J_n(x) + x^n J'_n(x) \amp = x^n J_{n-1} (x) \\ -nx^{-n-1} J_n(x) +x^{-n} J'_n(x) \amp = - x^{-n} J_{n+1}(x) \end{align*}
and multiply the first equation by \(x^{1-n}\) and the second by \(x^{1+n}\text{,}\) one gets
\begin{align*} n J_n + x J'_n \amp = xJ_{n-1} \\ -nJ_n+xJ'_n \amp = -xJ_{n+1} \end{align*}
Adding the two above equations and dividing through by \(x\) results in (7.7.6) whereas subtracting the bottom equation from the top results in (7.7.6).
