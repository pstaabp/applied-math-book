Remark 3.5.9.

Let \(\vec{u}\) be an element in a vector space \(S\) and \(( \vec{v}_1, \vec{v}_2, \ldots, \vec{v}_n )\) be an orthogonal basis of a subspace \(V\text{.}\) The projection of \(\vec{u}\) onto \(V\) can be written:
\begin{equation*} \text{Proj}_V (\vec{u}) = \sum_{i=1}^n c_k v_k \end{equation*}
where
\begin{equation*} c_k = \frac{\langle \vec{u}, \vec{v}_k \rangle}{\langle \vec{v}_k, \vec{v}_k \rangle} \end{equation*}
Note: if the basis of \(V\) is also orthonormal, then \(c_k = \langle \vec{u}, \vec{v}_k \rangle\text{.}\)
