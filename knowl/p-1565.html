The result of this theorem will save work if you have a real matrix and find a complex eigenvalue. For example in Example Example 4.1.5, the first eigenvalue-eigenvector pairs was \(\lambda_1=2i\) and \(\vec{v}_1=[-i \; \; 1]^{\intercal}\text{.}\) The above theorem shows that \(\lambda_2=\overline{\lambda_1} = -2i\) and \(\vec{v}_2=\overline{\vec{v}_1} = [i \; \; 1]^{\intercal}\) is another eigenvalue-eigenvector pair.
