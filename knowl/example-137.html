Example 6.1.12.

Show that \(f(x)=1/x\) is not a piecewise continuous function on \([-1,1]\text{.}\)
Solution.
The problem in this function on the interval \([-1,1]\) is at \(x=0\text{.}\) Also
\begin{align*} \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^-} f(x) \amp = -\infty \amp \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} f(x) \amp = \infty \end{align*}
and since the one-sided limits do not exist, \(f\) is not piecewise continuous on \([-1,1]\text{.}\)
