Solution 2.4.5.1.

In order to check this, then we try to find \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) such that
\begin{equation*} c_1 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} + c_2 \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
or the linear system
\begin{align*} c_1 + c_2 \amp = x_1\\ c_2 \amp = x_2 \\ c_1 \amp = x_3 \end{align*}
however from the latter two equations, there is a contradiction with the first equation which would say \(x_2+x_3 = x_1\) therefore there is no solution. This means that the two given vectors do not span \(\mathbb{R}^3\text{.}\)
