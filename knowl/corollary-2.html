Corollary 1.6.21. Cauchy-Swartz Inequality.

Let \(\vec{u}\) and \(\vec{v}\) be vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} | \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}| \leq ||\vec{u}|| \, ||\vec{v}|| \end{equation*}
with equality if and only if \(\vec{u}\) and \(\vec{v}\) are scalar multiples of each other.

Proof.

A consequence of the Triangle Inequality is ((1.6.5)), thus the Cauchy-Swartz Inequality holds if \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} \gt 0\text{.}\) Assume that \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} \lt 0\text{.}\) Then
\begin{align*} \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = - (\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}) = (-\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}) \amp \leq ||-\vec{u}|| ||\vec{v}|| \\ \text{and since} \; ||-\vec{u}|| = ||\vec{u}|| \qquad \amp \\ \amp = ||\vec{u}|| \, ||\vec{v}|| \end{align*}
For equality, assume that \(\vec{u} = k \vec{v}\) for some real number \(k\text{.}\) Then
\begin{align*} |\vec{u}\cdot \vec{v} | \amp = |k \vec{v} \cdot \vec{v}| \\ \amp = |k| \; |\vec{v} \cdot \vec{v} | \\ \amp = |k| \; || \vec{v} ||^2 \end{align*}
and since these steps are reversible, this hold for if and only if.
